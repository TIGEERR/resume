RESEARCH EXPERIENCE
Advisor: Prof. Yong Li, Tsinghua University Sep 2020 - July 2021, Beijing, China
We addressed the problem of predicting the consumption intention prediction, which is the internal driving force
of consumption behaviors. I contributed to design a detection model to label the users review data as intentions,
and implemented a multi-behavior graph convolution model to introduce the users intrinsic preference. We
improved the state-of-the-art performance of graph convolution methods on predicting consumption intention
task. This project underlines that the personalized interests is vital for the consumption intentions.
Advisor: Prof. Yong Li, Tsinghua University Sep 2021 - Feb 2022, Beijing, China
The recommender is biased towards popular items while ignoring those new items with little or no prior
interaction history. I proposed an unbiased pooling strategy in the pretrained model to eliminate the popularity
bias and generate unbiased pseudo interactions adaptively. Extensive experiments show that our approach
can signicantly and steadily outperform the state-of-the-art methods, both for popular and new items. On
the Amazon-Book dataset, where the long-tailed distribution is quite severe, we obtain 27.5% higher recall
compared to the baseline method and 9.6% higher than the state-of-the-art method.
Advisor: Prof. Julian McAuley, University of California San Diego Sep 2022 - present, CA, USA
We unied the conversation part and recommendation part in the conversational recommendation by generating
texts and items to the users simultaneously. This novel unied paradigm can promote the sharing of knowledge
between the two parts, and generate responses with more semantic consistency to the user in practice. We will
propose new metrics to evaluate language and recommendation capabilities of the models in real life scenarios.
